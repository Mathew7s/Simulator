Why These Simulators Matter for Head of Cloud CoE Role

As Head of Cloud Center of Excellence at UBS Card Center AG, you own four critical responsibilities: (1) establishing cloud governance standards aligned with UBS Group Azure strategy, (2) optimizing cloud infrastructure costs via FinOps discipline, (3) ensuring PCI-DSS/FINMA regulatory compliance in cloud environments, and (4) enabling rapid innovation in emerging payment modalities. These three simulators demonstrate how to approach each challenge strategically.

Cloud Governance & Regulatory Compliance

These tools show the CTO/CISO that you don't just "move to cloud"—you establish governance frameworks that prove compliance under audit. Each simulator models Azure governance maturity, compliance gates, audit evidence collection, and regulatory readiness timelines, demonstrating that you think holistically about shared responsibility model (what stays with UBS vs. Azure).

Financial Discipline & FinOps

UBS uses TBM (Technology Business Management) + FinOps practices company-wide. Your simulators prove you understand cost tracking by business unit, reserved capacity optimization, chargeback models, and ROI sensitivity analysis. This directly aligns with how UBS measures cloud program success.

Competitive Positioning Through Innovation

Card Center risks losing market share to fintech if not cloud-native. These simulators show you can quantify the business value of cloud investment: faster time-to-market for new payment products, revenue uplift from emerging modalities, and competitive advantage through cloud-enabled AI/fraud detection at scale.

Azure-First Strategy Alignment

UBS committed to running 50%+ of applications on Microsoft Azure. Your simulators use Azure-specific services (Azure SQL, Entra ID, DevOps, Defender for Cloud), demonstrating that you understand the group's strategic platform choice and how to operationalize it for payment processing.

For CTO: Cloud architecture standards, Azure governance, time-to-deploy
For CISO: PCI-DSS readiness, compliance gates, regulatory audit evidence
For CFO: Cloud costs, FinOps ROI, TCO savings, chargeback model
For Chief Cards Officer: Innovation velocity, market share, new revenue

Alignment with UBS Group Cloud Strategy

Azure as Primary Cloud Platform

These simulators are built on Microsoft Azure as the primary cloud platform, aligned with UBS Group's October 2022 strategic partnership announcement and commitment to run 50%+ of applications on Azure by 2025+. Simulators reference Azure-specific services: Azure SQL Database Hyperscale, Azure DevOps, Microsoft Entra ID, Azure Defender for Cloud, Azure Purview.

PCI-DSS & FINMA Compliance Framework

UBS Card Center must maintain PCI-DSS certification for payment processing and comply with FINMA regulatory requirements for Swiss banking. Simulators model compliance gates explicitly: data residency (CH + EU), encryption, audit trails, identity management (Entra ID), and regulatory approval timeline.

FinOps Discipline

UBS published case studies on "How UBS Is Using TBM and FinOps to Successfully Enable Their Cloud Journey." These simulators reflect FinOps best practices: cost tracking by business unit, reserved capacity optimization, chargeback models, and ROI sensitivity analysis.

Shared Responsibility Model

All simulators emphasize which controls stay with UBS (identity, data classification, compliance monitoring) vs. Azure (infrastructure, platform security, data center). This directly addresses FINMA guidance on cloud computing risk management.

📋 Regulatory Context: These simulators model compliance with FINMA Guidance 03/2024 (Cloud Computing), PCI-DSS 4.0, ISO 27001, DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act), and GDPR. all benchmarks are from public sources.
